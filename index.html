The Challenge

The Problem No One Is Addressing

African internet users face unique digital security challenges that many in developed markets simply don't experience. The consequences of data breaches, phishing attacks, SIM swaps, and identity theft are devastating — and they affect millions daily.

No Local Solutions

Existing security tools are expensive, not localized, and not designed for African realities.

Data Breaches

Personal data exposed in breaches affects job seekers, students, and families across the continent.

Identity Theft

Stolen identities lead to financial loss, reputational damage, and emotional distress.

SIM Swaps

Criminals exploit weak verification systems to hijack phone numbers and drain accounts.

This isn't theoretical. This is real. Every day, Africans go online without the protection they deserve. We're building the solution.

The Reality

What's Limiting Me Right Now

Building world-class infrastructure with zero capital is possible... but slow. Every limitation below adds months to the timeline.

Limited Internet

Data in Nigeria costs significantly more than developed markets. Can't run continuous campaigns or test at scale. Video streaming and large uploads are costly.

No Marketing Capital

Tirenify grows only through organic word-of-mouth. Can't run paid campaigns or strategic partnerships. User acquisition is limited by what I can share manually.

Building Solo

All product development, marketing, and strategy falls on one person. Can't build and market simultaneously. Ideas move to execution slowly.

Free Infrastructure

Currently at tirenify.netlify.app. Using free hosting with limited features. No custom domain — looks amateur to potential users and limits professional appearance.

No Paid Tools

Using free Claude tier with limited tokens. Free tier of every tool. Can't invest in faster services. Development iteration is slow — every feature takes longer.

None of these are insurmountable. But together, they create artificial constraints. Capital removes these bottlenecks.
